Trigonometric Ratios

 

The trigonometric ratios are special measurements of a right triangle (a triangle with one angle measuring 90°.) The three basic trigonometric ratios are sine, cosine, and tangent. Given any right triangle, you can find the sine, cosine, or tangent of either of the non-90° angles. The three other common trigonometric ratios are secant, cosecant, and secant. They utilize the three sides that make up any right triangle: the opposite, hypotenuse, and adjacent side.

Sinusoidal Functions

 

The general equation for a sinusoidal function is: f(x) = ±a•sin( b(x+c)+d. The ± controls the reflection across the x axis, coefficient a controls the amplitude, the constant d controls the vertical shift, and in this equation, the coefficient b controls the horizontal stretch.

 

The period of a sinusoid is the length of a complete cycle. For basic sine and cosine functions, the period is 2𝝅.
